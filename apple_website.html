(02) Project Overview

(a) Research Question

How do you determine the variety of apples most suited to someone’s specific preferences?

(b) Relation to Data Science

We will use data science techniques to analyze datasets of apple flavor properties and map them to user‑stated preferences.

(c) Why This Matters

Apples are popular, but not all apples taste the same. Matching apples better helps people discover apples they’ll genuinely enjoy—and supports growers, retailers, and food waste reduction.

Highlights

  • Preference modeling for sweetness, tartness, crispness, juiciness, and aroma.
  • Evidence‑based suggestions grounded in public datasets.
  • Path to an interactive recommender (web app or dashboard).

(03) Data Sources

Primary and secondary datasets used to characterize apple varieties and quantify sensory attributes.

(a) Primary Dataset(s)

Source: Website — Pomiferous

Description: Catalog of apple varieties with qualitative descriptors and characteristics.

Size/Scope: Over 7,000 apple types across 7 harvest periods.

(b) Secondary Dataset(s)

Source: Kaggle — Apple Quality

Description: Numeric measurements related to flavor/texture useful for modeling preferences.

Reference (Flavor Chart)

Rittman Orchards Apple Chart — quick, human‑readable flavor & use notes to cross‑check attributes.

(04) Planned Approach

We’ll refine as the project develops.

(a) Analysis Methods

  • Exploratory data analysis & summary statistics
  • Visualization techniques (heatmaps, scatter plots, parallel coordinates)
  • Feature engineering for sensory axes (e.g., sweetness ↔ tartness, crispness)
  • Similarity search / simple recommender (k‑NN) and, if useful, lightweight ML models

(b) Expected Visualizations

  • Interactive dashboard showing preference sliders → top‑N apple suggestions
  • Correlation heatmap of attributes
  • Scatter/UMAP to show clusters of varieties

(c) Tools & Technologies

  • Languages: Python (or R)
  • Libraries: pandas, numpy, scikit‑learn, matplotlib/Plotly, altair
  • Viz: Streamlit / Plotly Dash (for web app)
